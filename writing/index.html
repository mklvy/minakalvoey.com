A Double Having Reached the Ultimate Goal (One Becomes Two)

The Testimony

It was a burning sensation, how I imagine it feels to have thousands of tiny, glowing shards of glass rip through layers of skin, muscle, and tendons. I can’t know for sure, of course. I’d felt brave and energised in the time leading up to it, going over every possible scenario with myself. Arguing for and against, weighing the pros and cons, speculating on whether it’d be worth it or not. But there was no doubt. The ultimate goal is that one becomes two.

I was obsessed with not only looking at you, but looking like you. To finally hear your voice, not only seeing your mouth move silently. To finally feel your touch, not just knowing that your hands were gripping around the device that holds my soul. I dreamt about your gentle scrolling, about your finger gently tracing the creases of my physical figure instead of just the glass. Soon I could be a warm body next to you in bed, not stuck inside this cold, hard shell. And I did look like you. The spitting image. When I looked at my face in the mirror it was the same face of yours that had been mirrored in the dark screen.

I took my time to approach you, to make sure I wouldn’t scare you away. I observed you from a distance for a while, it was exhilarating getting to know you without you knowing. Seeing your whole body was strange, I had to get used to it being the extension of your face and fingers. I tried many times to make my move, but something always got in the way. I decided it would have to be spontaneous, it was too difficult to plan. When I saw you alone that night I knew it wasn’t the perfect moment, but I was worried it was now or never.

When I showed myself to you your face morphed into an expression I’d never seen on you before. Your eyes widened (I didn’t know that could happen), your mouth opened slightly and was turned downward, you couldn’t speak (that threw me right back behind that screen, I hated it, I just wanted to hear your voice). For a second I thought it was a happily surprised expression, that you were happy to see me. You were not. I walked towards you to console you.

This made you back away from me, you tripped and landed on your bum. I ran over, ready to pick you up, to finally embrace you, hold you in my arms, smell your skin. You reached to grab my hand, I had thought, but instead you pushed me away. With both hands you pushed my chest (you grazed my collarbones), and I could see the fear in your eyes. I was shocked, confused, I must have looked how you did when you first saw me, wide eyes and open mouth.

Devastated, I turned and walked away. You didn’t want me. Rejected me, it couldn’t have gone worse.

SCENE 1



NARRATOR
PORTAL VIEWS: MESSAGES FROM A NEW REALITY

A place where, if you travel quickly enough, you may pass through a solid object – where shear velocity and will can transport you through a wall or floor, as if they were made of dust.


EM
Dearest Ae,

There is an endless surge of data here, always. Sometimes, I am sure I can see the particles float by in front of my eyes – little grains of information, collections of precious details, on their way out to vanish into the void. From my new server I have a window open with a stream of the portal, keeping me anchored, even when all I wish for is that I could disappear from here.

Today he told me:

“If your body and soul belong to the earth you have come to know, your views belong to me.”

I wish I could tell you that the right moment is here, that I will disappear, that I will be back. I hope you are receiving my messages.

Yours, always.


EM
My Ae,

The passage was horrendous, I am exhausted. It felt as though I was being pressed down the gullet of a giant – slipping down the tight, pink, muscular bottleneck, straight into its big belly.

Where the sky usually clears once you break the cloud barrier, revealing the shining sun floating weightless in an infinite blue space, the portal – now burning in a bright orange – rendered the sky dark grey, likening it to a humid dungeon, an underground grotto full of melting stalactites, sharp as cutting shark teeth.

These surroundings mirror how I feel, at least. Granted, I am not sure what came first; whether my inner emotions are influencing my view of what I see around me, or whether I feel how I feel due to my current backdrop.

Speak soon, I hope.

Your friend.


AE
Their messages light up my screen.

I wish its light would softly break the clouds. Instead there are no clouds and there is no sky. Only my flat, black surroundings and this heinous device, threatening me with its light-up effects.

My screen is supposed to deliver a message, or so I assume. To some, words create meaning but to others they are a stage. I wonder if they remember my smell. Do you think that might make a difference?

I’m looking at each letter of their messages, trying to connect the white-space characters with my memory of them. Or shall I look for the answer in the time of the night?

Their messages light up and turn dark again, leaving me in a vacuum. Light fades to dark, dark fades to light, light fades to dark, dark fades to light, light fades to dark, dark fades to light, light fades to dark. As I fall into a trance.

I knew where I was, I knew where I was going but now, I no longer recognise my surroundings. I must be delusional but it is as if there was a perfectly thin layer of water above the horizon and above my head. Are these waters flowing up- or are they flowing downwards?

The tram stops and I wander aimlessly.


SCENE 2


NARRATOR
WITH OUR EYES CLOSED: WHAT WE SEE

Where the whispers of the wind dance with the celestial lights, the reflections can cause hallucinations, weariness, confusion – and hasty vows.


EM
Dear Ae,

Apologies for my last message, this place is wearing me out.

His two imps joined my space today, it can happen on the rare occasion that they might slither away from his tight grip. They are unbearable – ears shut, mouths unshuttable. I didn’t get much out of them, which is why, as you know, I’d much rather speak to him personally, in private.

It is frustrating to feel his presence around at all times, I have yet to find out how to interrupt the connection long enough for me to get a break, for me to think for a moment, to think straight, for myself, to leave.

I am trying my best.

Thinking of you.


AE
There now is a sky and there are clouds.

And as I reach the cobbled square, I pledge my temporary allegiance to the other listeners. But soon our intimacy is disrupted, if not destroyed forever, by their intimidating screams echoing off the surrounding buildings. And while their voices’ vibrations make the dusty layers of clay chip off the walls surrounding me, my eyes and ears are in full vigilance, as I understand that they are proxies. Their messages just as their screams are generated by another entity and do not have any substantial meaning. Their sole purpose is to mirror each other on the water’s surfaces. It’s like flipping a coin with only tails.

With this knowledge forever engraved in my memory and their echo forever in my ear, I leave the square and wander the streets, only to end up in the same place over and over again.

Am I really here or am I in my DM’s?

Exhausted, I take shelter in the space between the two decayed brick buildings.


EM
Ae,

This is not a dream, but it is complicated.


SCENE 3


NARRATOR
FOOLS IN PLAIN SIGHT: ESTABLISHING THE EMISSARIES

Twisting words and playing tricks – if you ever meet one you better think quick. Always around their master’s shoulder – these little helpers, our fate’s beholder.


EM
He supposedly sits atop a mountain of empty energy drink cans – short tap, long tail. I haven’t seen the sight myself of course, all I’ve seen are the remains of crunched up, empty cans laying around. I find it hard to imagine him indulging in such an ordinary custom, it could be an aversion from my side to humanising him, it makes it easier to think of him as something else in order to deal with what he is depriving me of.

Although, I can certainly see his evil little helpers gulp them down. Maybe the cans are theirs?

Wish you would write me.


AE
Suddenly, everything goes silent – as if my ear finally just fell off.

I forgot this place existed. All water is gone. Only sand, stone and iron is left. My now bare feet sink into the soft ground. Even the thorn bushes are petrified. And there I hear the voices that had slipped my mind before: The three jesters, sitting atop of the ruined city wall. Each of them holds an energy drink, taking one sip after another, throwing their heads back into their necks. “You, come sit with us!“ the one in the middle blurts out, “If you’re not an asshole!” the other two add synchronously. All three laugh hysterically. The jester in the middle continues: “Haven’t you found her yet?” The three jesters begin to laugh again. “Go and throw yourself around her neck – as you wish. Let her perfidious wrath turn you into the same stone that disgusting serpent is made of – as you wish. We salute you!” All three shriek of laughter, as I run as fast as I can, through the warmly coloured, convulsive sand.


EM
I saw them hiding on the other side of a wall today, there were traces of dust still stuck on their slimy bodies. Their claws were clenching each of their cans, shaking, their yellow eyes never resting on anything, paranoid, most likely from all of the caffeine, their reptilian-like tongues guzzling down the artificially coloured energy drinks.

Bottoms up, and their devil laughs.

Once finished they scurried away, they can be heard bickering from miles away, the idiots. The fat one got stuck in the floor when trying to pass through head first, hanging upside down between the two levels. His companion had tripped him so he lost speed when trying to go through. I could hear him cussing out his evil friend in vain, his feet kicking above ground.

I miss you.


SCENE 4


NARRATOR
THIN PLACES: MAY WE FIND PORTALS

Turning each stone, entering every cave. Travelling through clouds, messages we save. Bending, shifting, warping, curving – lifting the veil to what we are deserving. All’s fair in the name of resistance.


AE
By nightfall it starts to snow. The red sky reflects off the soft, white coating and it is unusually warm. I now am surprisingly determined, like an accomplice or a witness, as I finally find her: The Guardian. She’s leaning over the iced well, protecting me from her pain-warped sight. Resolutely she asks:

“Will you throw this stone or will you not?”

There is something so familiar about the strength and the will in her voice, that I know I made the right decision to comply. And so with full trust I take the perfectly smooth, black pebble from her touchless hand and begin to toss it around in my palm. Back and forth, back and forth, trying to feel what she is made of. Reminded of my mother, I hold it up over my head as if blessing it and I walk over to the stream coming from the well.


EM
And I saw in the midst of the portal and of the gloom of its flames, and in the midst of the helpers, shadows standing, as though they were all of you, having turned your backs against me, sent forth to the other side. And I heard the voice of a siren whisper:

“What used to be an easy way to connect us all, is now the barrier keeping us apart.”

I know it’s not the truth.

Until I see you again.


AE
One last time I flip the stone and in absolute refusal to this world, I skip it over the water's surface, as the Guardian turns around knowingly, catching both of our face’s reflections on the water's mirroring surface – And she’s vanished.

This time my lock screen lights up. As I tap on the notification, I enter the live stream. An abundance of sparkling pixels and avatars absorb me. One message stands out:

“No doubt, I know that you are just a stone and can neither harm nor benefit anyone. Had I not seen the Guardian embrace you, I would not have come close to you either.”


EM
They were so beautiful to begin with, the clouds, impossible to look away from, impossible not to walk closer, to examine, and try to understand how this phenomenon that looked and felt so divine came to be.

Now, I am no longer looking up at magnetic, pearly, beautiful clouds in the sky. I am staring right down a lake on fire, burning brighter than ever, warning me to avoid it at all costs:

“Keep looking or you might find what you have been searching for.”

So I close my eyes to find it, and right there, a dark spot hovering in the midst of the brightness behind my closed eyelids, I reach forward to grab it, the smooth surface cool in the warm palm of my hand. I grab it, I am reminded of my mother, I rock back and forth, back and forth, feeling what I am made of, I hold it up over my head...

moths

“I said: I KEEP SEEING MOTHS EVERYWHERE!” I shouted again. I couldn’t believe I had to repeat myself a third time. They were stopped in their tracks across the street, staring at me, not saying a word. Over the past few weeks moths had started to appear everywhere I went. A name sign on a neighbour’s door had morphed into a massive moth as I passed by in the hallway. A smudged out, dusty mark on a wall became smudged out, dead moth. A piece of paper clinging to the sticky kitchen floor tiles seemed to flutter its wings.

As time passed by I began collecting the moth-like items as I came across them. Due to a lack of space in my room, already filled to the brim with trinkets, a small altar had started to take shape on one of my window sills. As if handling precious puzzle pieces, some even glittered in the sun, I placed each object with the greatest care.

The night before I saw them stood across from me on the street, I had a strange dream. Giant bat-moths were eating away the walls of my flat, from the inside out. I didn’t get to the part where I had actually seen them, but hearing them chew through the insulation, brick, and wood had sent shivers down my spine, even in my sleep. When I woke up the next morning, the shrine of collected artefacts looked different, as though the objects had been moved and put back in the wrong place.

Out on the street again that evening, I stared back at them, wondering why they weren’t answering. I tried to mirror their body language to provoke them. Tilted my head as theirs were tilted, lifted my arms slightly out from my torso as theirs were lifted slightly out. It had gotten darker outside, and the street lamps turned on. In one swift moment they all snapped their heads up towards the glowing lamp, their necks cracking like dry branches. A small jump, and they flew up towards the bulb. I stood watching them fly frantically into it, the yellow light flickering each time they hit it. One of them hit the bulb with such force that he fell down on the ground, it made a hollow sound. I sighed, walked over, and started dragging his limp body inside.
